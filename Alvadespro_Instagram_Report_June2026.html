What Went Well

🚀

Organic is Back — Views More Than Doubled

After two months of falling numbers, June brought a strong recovery. Views went from 1,100 to 2,100 (+114.7%). Reach jumped from 564 to 1,100 (+94.5%). Adding questions to captions and posting strong content early in the month both contributed to this turnaround.

💬

First DM Conversation — A New Signal

One conversation was started this month. This is small but important. It means someone was interested enough to reach out directly. As organic content improves, this number should grow into real leads.

💰

Ads Stay Efficient — All Benchmarks Still Beat

For the third month in a row, CTR (4.60%), CPM ($13.60), and CPC ($0.32) all beat the US industry benchmark. The ads cost far less per click than the typical US home design ad.

What Needs to Improve

⚠️

Ad 1 CTR Dropped — Creative Fatigue is Setting In

Ad 1 CTR fell from 7.47% (May) to 6.44% (June). Total ad reach dropped from 10.9K to 8.1K and link clicks fell from 735 to 612. After 3 months of running the same ads, the audience has seen them before. New creative is urgent.

💬

Zero Comments — Three Months in a Row

Despite views doubling, not one comment was left on any post in June. Views and reach are growing but the audience is still not talking. Stories polls and a reply-worthy question format need to be tested in July.

🔗

Zero Conversions from Ads — No Orders Created

The dashboard shows 0 orders created. Hundreds of people are clicking the ads but no one is buying or reaching out. The landing page or bio link experience after the click needs to be reviewed and improved.

5 Priority Actions for July 2026

01

Launch New Ad Creative — The Current Ads Are Aging

Ad 1 has been running since April — three months on the same creative. Create at least one new video ad with a fresh visual angle and the same strong message about luxury design vs AI. Test it against the current ads in July.

02

Fix the Post-Click Experience — Why Are People Not Converting?

Over 600 people clicked the ads in June and zero became leads or orders. Check the bio link, landing page, or WhatsApp flow after the click. Make the next step simple and clear: one button, one action, one offer.

03

Add Stories Polls Every Week to Break the Zero-Comment Pattern

Polls are a one-tap interaction. Run a simple poll every week like "Warm tones or cool tones?" This trains the audience to engage with the account and warms them up to comment on posts.

04

Keep the Question-in-Caption Format — It Doubled Views

Views went from 643 to 1,267 in one month after adding questions to captions. This format must stay in every post. Keep the question short, specific, and easy to answer.

05

Create One "Saveable" Carousel Per Week

Saves are up (2 this month). Create at least one Carousel per week with a list format like "5 Signs Your Home Needs a Design Update" or "3 Things Every Luxury Kitchen Has." These get saved and shared more than any other format.

Final Note
June 2026 is a breakthrough month for organic content — the biggest growth since the account was active. The paid ads are still highly efficient but showing early signs of fatigue. July's job is clear: refresh the ads, fix the post-click funnel, and turn viewers into commenters. If those three things happen, the account is set up for its strongest quarter yet.
